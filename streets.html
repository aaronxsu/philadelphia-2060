18: Require Only Off-Street Parking

On-street parking capacity can be replaced through more space-efficient garages. Utilizing former street parking space for other purposes – both public space and transportation – will allow streets to better serve people.

X

19: Ban Private Cars in the Urban Core Zone

Center City and University City are the historic, cultural, and economic centers of Philadelphia.

Removing private cars from the Urban Core Zone will create an inviting public realm, while still efficiently moving people in, out, and through Philadelphia’s core.

X

20: Create a Network of Separated-Mode Arterials

To reclaim street space for public spaces, moving streets must allow for the quick travel of AVs and surface transit. They will be the primary means of travel across the city. To ensure safety and efficiency on high-speed arterials, modes will be separated.

X

21: Allow for Shared-Mode Residential and Commercial Lanes

The injuries and fatalities that affect minority and low-income areas at higher rates can be drastically reduced by allowing slow speed, shared-mode streets in residential and commercial areas.

X

22: Utilize Reclaimed Right-of-Way as Public Space

By reclaiming space in the right-of-way, a significant amount of land can be used to create new public spaces that prioritize people. Neighborhoods across the city, especially those without good access to public space will benefit from these additional spaces.

X

23: Design Environmentally Responsive Streets

Philadelphia has the opportunity to utilize green infrastructure, vegetation, and pervious surface to better incorporate streets into the natural environment. Areas that have experienced issues with heat and pollution will be prioritized.

X

24: Facilitate Shipping and Deliveries

Streets will need to facilitate deliveries that are not disruptive to traffic. Alternative modes of delivery can occur at any time off the sidewalk. High and medium density streets will provide space for delivery vehicles and pedestrian loading.
